## How I Lead

> *"The best platforms are built by teams that trust each other. My job is to create the conditions, the clarity, the safety, and the momentum, that make great engineering possible."*

**Build for durability, not the sprint.**
I have a strong bias toward foundations that outlast their first use case. Every platform I've launched (MAPLE, CAMP, UMR, Data Acceleration) was designed to be adopted well beyond its original scope. Good platforms multiply team leverage. They don't create dependencies.

**AI strategy is business strategy.**
Getting an LLM into production is a solved problem. Getting an LLM platform that an entire org trusts and builds on top of is not. That gap is where I do my best work, and it requires judgment as much as technical skill.

**Scale the humans, not just the systems.**
Growing an org from 12 to 25+ engineers is not just a headcount exercise. The hardest decisions are about team structure, hiring bars, and how the team makes decisions under pressure. I've done it without sacrificing quality or culture, and I think a lot about why that's hard.
